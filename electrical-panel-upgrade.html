Signs Your Home Needs a Panel Upgrade

Recognizing when your electrical panel can no longer safely handle your home's demands helps you address problems before they become dangerous. Several warning signs indicate that your Las Vegas home needs an electrical panel upgrade.

Frequent Circuit Breaker Trips

If circuit breakers trip regularly, especially when running air conditioning and other appliances simultaneously, your electrical panel lacks adequate capacity. Breakers trip to protect your home from dangerous overloads. Frequent tripping means your electrical demands exceed your panel's capacity. This isn't just inconvenient—it indicates a safety issue that requires attention.

Dimming or Flickering Lights

Lights that dim when you start appliances indicate voltage drops from inadequate electrical capacity. This happens when your electrical panel struggles to distribute power to all circuits simultaneously. Flickering lights show unstable power distribution that can damage sensitive electronics and appliances.

Warm or Hot Electrical Panel

An electrical panel should never feel warm or hot to the touch. Heat indicates dangerous overloading or failing components. A hot panel represents an active fire hazard that requires immediate professional attention. Never ignore a panel that feels warm—call for electrical service right away.

Inability to Add New Circuits

If you want to add an electric vehicle charger, hot tub, or major appliance but your panel has no available breaker spaces, you need an upgrade. Even if spaces are available, your panel may lack the electrical capacity to support additional loads. We calculate your total electrical load to determine if your panel can safely handle additions.

Old or Obsolete Panel Types

Homes with Federal Pacific, Zinsco, or other obsolete panel brands should be upgraded immediately. These panels have known safety issues and may not protect your home properly. Insurance companies often require replacement of these dangerous panels. Our electricians identify problematic panel types and recommend appropriate replacements.

Understanding Electrical Panel Capacity

Electrical panels are rated by their amperage capacity, which determines how much electrical power they can safely distribute. Understanding panel capacity helps you recognize when upgrades are necessary for your Las Vegas home.

100-Amp Electrical Panels

Most Las Vegas homes built before 1990 have 100-amp electrical panels. These panels provided adequate capacity when homes had fewer appliances and smaller air conditioning systems. Today, a 100-amp panel struggles to handle modern electrical demands, especially during Las Vegas summers when air conditioners run constantly.

A 100-amp panel can safely provide about 19,200 watts of power. This might sound like a lot, but a central air conditioner uses 3,000 to 5,000 watts, an electric water heater uses 4,000 watts, an electric range uses 3,000 watts, and an electric dryer uses 3,000 watts. Add computers, televisions, and other devices, and you quickly approach or exceed the panel's capacity.

200-Amp Electrical Panels

Modern homes require 200-amp electrical service. This capacity safely handles air conditioning, kitchen appliances, laundry equipment, electronics, and allows room for future additions like electric vehicle chargers. A 200-amp panel provides about 38,400 watts of available power—double the capacity of a 100-amp panel.

Upgrading from 100-amp to 200-amp service eliminates frequent breaker trips and voltage problems. You can run your air conditioning, use kitchen appliances, and charge your electric vehicle simultaneously without electrical issues. The additional capacity also supports home additions and upgrades without further electrical work.

Calculating Your Electrical Load

Our electricians calculate your home's total electrical load to determine the right panel size. This calculation includes all major appliances, heating and cooling systems, lighting, and planned additions. We ensure your new panel has adequate capacity plus reserve for future needs. Proper load calculation prevents installing a panel that's still too small for your requirements.

The Panel Upgrade Process Explained

Understanding what happens during an electrical panel upgrade helps you prepare for the project and know what to expect. Our systematic approach ensures safe, code-compliant installations that pass inspection the first time.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

The upgrade process begins with our experienced electrician inspecting your current electrical system. We examine your existing panel, evaluate its condition, check for code violations, and identify any problems with your electrical wiring. We measure your current electrical load and discuss your future electrical needs.

After the inspection, we provide a detailed proposal explaining what work is needed and why. You receive clear pricing that includes all permits, materials, labor, and inspection fees. We answer your questions and ensure you understand the entire process before work begins.

Permits and Utility Coordination

Electrical panel upgrades require permits from your local building department. We handle all permit applications and paperwork. Panel upgrades also require coordination with your utility company to disconnect and reconnect power. We schedule utility service and ensure minimal disruption to your daily routine.

The permit process typically takes one to three days depending on your local jurisdiction. We don't start work until all permits are approved. This ensures your upgrade meets all code requirements and passes final inspection.

Installation Day Process

Panel upgrade installation typically takes six to eight hours. Our electricians arrive at the scheduled time with all necessary materials and equipment. We start by documenting your existing panel configuration and labeling all circuits so we know where everything connects.

Power Shutdown and Old Panel Removal

The utility company temporarily disconnects power to your home at the meter. This allows us to work safely on your electrical service entrance. We then carefully remove your old panel, documenting and photographing all connections. This documentation ensures proper reconnection in the new panel.

New Panel Installation

We install the new 200-amp panel in the same location or relocate it if needed for code compliance or convenience. The installation includes mounting the new panel securely, connecting the service entrance cables, installing the main breaker, and installing individual circuit breakers for all your home's circuits.

We reconnect each circuit to the appropriate breaker, ensuring proper wire sizing and protection. All connections are made according to current electrical codes. We also upgrade the grounding system if needed to meet current requirements. Proper grounding is essential for electrical safety and protects against shocks and surges.

System Testing and Verification

After installation, we test every circuit to verify proper operation. We check voltage at outlets throughout your home, verify that all switches and outlets work correctly, and test GFCI outlets. Load testing ensures the panel can handle your electrical demands without problems.

We show you the new panel and explain how it works. You'll learn which breakers control which circuits, how to reset a tripped breaker, and when to call for service. Clear labeling makes it easy to identify circuits when needed.

Inspection and Final Approval

City electrical inspections verify that panel upgrades meet all safety codes and are installed correctly. We coordinate all inspections and ensure your upgrade passes on the first attempt.

What Inspectors Check

Electrical inspectors examine the panel installation, verify proper wire sizing, check all connections, confirm adequate grounding, and ensure code compliance. They also verify that the installation matches the approved permit. Our experience with local inspectors and code requirements ensures your upgrade meets all standards.

After Inspection Completion

Once the inspection passes, you receive official documentation showing your electrical system is code-compliant. This documentation is important for insurance purposes and future home sales. We provide copies of all permits, inspection certificates, and warranty information.

If exterior work was needed to install the new panel, we repair any damage to stucco, siding, or drywall. We clean the work area thoroughly and remove all debris. Your home is left clean with a fully functional, upgraded electrical system.

Benefits of Upgrading Your Electrical Panel

Electrical panel upgrades provide numerous benefits beyond simply having more electrical capacity. Understanding these advantages helps you appreciate the value of this important home improvement.

Improved Safety

Modern electrical panels include better circuit protection and safety features. We install proper circuit breakers in all upgraded panels. They respond faster to overloads and ground faults, protecting your home from electrical fires. Updated panels eliminate the fire hazards associated with old, overloaded electrical systems. This protection gives you peace of mind knowing your family and property are safe.

Increased Home Value

Homes with updated electrical systems are more attractive to buyers and typically sell for higher prices. Buyers appreciate knowing they won't need to invest in electrical upgrades immediately after purchase. Updated electrical systems also pass home inspections more easily, preventing issues during the sale process.

Lower Insurance Costs

Some insurance companies reduce premiums for homes with updated electrical systems. Old electrical panels increase the risk of electrical fires, leading to higher insurance rates. An updated panel demonstrates that your home meets current safety standards and presents lower risk.

Support for Modern Technology

Today's homes depend on technology that requires reliable electrical power. Computers, home offices, entertainment systems, and smart home devices all need stable electricity. An adequate electrical panel ensures these systems operate reliably without power issues that damage equipment or cause data loss.

Electric Vehicle Charging

Electric vehicle ownership is growing rapidly, and home charging provides the most convenient option. Installing an EV charger requires a dedicated 240-volt circuit that draws significant power. Many 100-amp panels cannot support EV chargers along with existing electrical loads. Upgrading to 200-amp service makes home EV charging possible.

Do I need a permit to upgrade my electrical panel?

Yes, electrical panel upgrades require permits from your local building department in Las Vegas. We handle all permit applications and paperwork for you. Permits ensure the work meets safety codes and passes inspection. Never hire contractors who suggest skipping permits—this creates serious legal and safety issues that can affect insurance coverage and home sales.

Can I upgrade my own electrical panel?

We strongly recommend against DIY electrical panel upgrades. Panel work involves high-voltage connections that can cause fatal electrocution. Improper installations create fire hazards and code violations. experienced electricians have the training, tools, and experience to upgrade panels safely. Insurance companies and building departments require experienced professionals for panel work.

Will upgrading my panel increase my electric bill?

No, upgrading your electrical panel does not increase your electric bill. The panel upgrade provides more capacity to distribute power safely, but it doesn't use more electricity. You'll only see higher bills if you add new appliances or devices that consume more power. Many homeowners actually save money because upgraded panels eliminate inefficiencies from overloaded circuits.

Signs you need a panel upgrade include breakers that trip frequently, lights that dim when appliances start, a panel that feels warm to touch, inability to add circuits for new appliances, or having a 100-amp panel in a modern home.
