Common Problems

Common Circuit Breaker Problems We Fix in Las Vegas

Breaker That Keeps Tripping Without Explanation

One that trips once is usually fine. One that keeps tripping is not.

It could be an overloaded circuit. It could be a short in the wiring. It could be a bad breaker that's worn out. We find out which one — then we fix the right thing.

Every call we take starts with a proper diagnosis. Not just a part swap.

Breaker That Feels Hot or Makes a Buzzing Noise

A healthy breaker runs cool. If yours is hot to the touch, that's a problem. Same goes for buzzing or crackling sounds from the panel.

Heat and noise mean the breaker is under stress. A loose connection, a failing breaker, or too much load — all of these can cause it. Left alone, this leads to arcing. Arcing causes fires.

Call us the same day you notice it. We respond quickly across the valley.

Breaker That Won't Reset or Stay On

You flip it off. You flip it back on. It trips right away.

This means one of three things. There's still a fault on the circuit. The breaker has failed inside. Or your panel has a deeper issue. We check all three before we leave.

Root Causes

Causes of Circuit Breaker Failure in Las Vegas Homes

Overloaded Circuits in Summer Heat

Summers here are brutal. AC runs all day. Add a microwave, TV, and other devices to the same circuit — it trips. Every time.

Older homes here weren't built for today's loads. When circuit breaker repair Las Vegas calls go up in July and August, overloaded circuits are almost always the cause.

The real fix isn't just swapping the breaker. It's adding a dedicated circuit for heavy devices.

Short Circuits & Ground Faults

A short circuit happens when a hot wire touches a neutral wire. The current spikes. The breaker trips right away. A ground fault does the same thing.

Both cause one symptom — the breaker trips every time you reset it. We use diagnostic tools to find the fault fast. We fix it and confirm the circuit is clean before we leave.

Aging or Defective Breaker Brands

Federal Pacific and Zinsco panels — common in homes here from the 1960s to 1980s — have a known failure problem. Their breakers sometimes don't trip when they should. That's a fire risk.

If we find one on a circuit breaker repair Las Vegas visit, we flag it and walk you through your options. It's a documented safety issue, not a sales pitch.

Breaker Types

Types of Circuit Breakers We Replace in Las Vegas

Standard Single-Pole & Double-Pole Breakers

Single-pole breakers cover 120-volt circuits — lights, outlets, small appliances. Double-pole breakers cover 240-volt circuits — dryers, AC units, EV chargers.

Both replaced across all major panel brands. Square D, Eaton, Siemens, Leviton, Cutler-Hammer — we stock them all for circuit breaker repair Las Vegas jobs.

AFCI & GFCI Breaker Installation

AFCI and GFCI breakers are required in specific areas of local homes under current code. Bedrooms, kitchens, bathrooms, garages, and outdoor circuits all have rules.

If yours is missing or won't reset, we handle it on the same visit.

Tandem & Specialty Breaker Replacement

Some panels use tandem breakers — two in one slot. These are brand-specific. We know the rules and carry the right stock for Las Vegas panel brands.
