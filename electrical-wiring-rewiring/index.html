Warning Signs

Signs Your Las Vegas Home Needs Rewiring

Flickering Lights & Tripping Breakers

Lights that flicker on their own point to loose wires or bad circuits. A breaker that trips on the same circuit again and again is a wiring problem. Not a breaker problem. The wire behind the wall is the issue.

Burning or Plastic Smell from Outlets or Walls

A burning smell near an outlet is a red flag. So is a plastic smell you can't trace to any appliance. These smells come from wire coating that gets too hot inside the wall. Stop using that outlet. Call us right away.

Outlets or Switches That Spark or Feel Warm

Outlets that spark often are a sign. Switches that feel warm — same. Outlet covers that are stained or dark — the wiring behind them is failing.

Aluminum Wiring Repair & Swap

Homes built from the late 1960s to mid-1970s often have aluminum wiring. An electrical rewiring Las Vegas check finds it fast. Aluminum moves more than copper. Over time, joints get loose. Loose joints arc. Arcing starts fires.

We fix aluminum wiring by adding copper at every joint. Or we replace whole sections if the condition is bad.

Know the Risks

Wiring Hazards in Older Las Vegas Homes

Why Knob & Tube Wiring Is Dangerous

Knob and tube is the oldest type we see. For any electrical rewiring Las Vegas job involving this, we document and replace it fully. It has no ground wire. The coating sits in hot wall spaces for 60 or more years. The heat here breaks it down fast.

Most insurers in Nevada won't cover a home with active knob and tube wiring.

Aluminum Wiring in 1970s Las Vegas Homes

Every outlet and switch on aluminum wiring is a real hot spot risk. We see it all the time in homes from that era. Fixed right.
