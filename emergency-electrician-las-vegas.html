Electrical Emergencies We Handle in Las Vegas

Sparking or Smoking Outlets & Switches

Sparks from an outlet or switch are a fire risk. Stop using it right away. Don't cover it. Don't assume it's fine. Call us. Don't leave this overnight.

The cause is usually a loose wire, a bad outlet, or a heavy circuit. All fixable. Not something to leave overnight.

Tripped Breakers That Won't Reset

A breaker that trips and resets is doing its job. One that trips again right away is different. One that won't reset is telling you there's a problem. Don't keep flipping it.

We find the fault and fix it. Same visit. As an emergency electrician Las Vegas team, that's the standard.

Complete Power Loss in Las Vegas Homes

Lost power to part of your home? Check your panel first. A tripped breaker or blown fuse may be the cause.

Lost power to the whole house? Check with NV Energy first — it may be a utility issue. If they confirm power is on at the meter and your house is still dark, call us.

That's a wiring or panel issue.

Electrical Burning Smell — What to Do Immediately

A burning smell near an outlet, switch, or panel is serious. Turn off that circuit at the breaker. Don't use the outlet. Open a window if the smell is strong.

Call an emergency electrician Las Vegas homes count on right away.

Burning smells come from arcing wires, overloaded circuits, or failing devices. All of these can start a fire. Don't wait.

Overloaded Circuits in High-Heat Las Vegas Summers

AC units, fans, and cooling gear run nonstop here in summer. That load adds up. Older panels weren't built for it. Circuits trip. Wires run hot. Risk goes up when it all runs at once.

Wiring Failure in Older Las Vegas Properties

Homes built before 1990 often have aluminum wiring or old cloth-wrapped wire. It breaks down. Connections loosen. Arcing starts. That's where fires begin.
