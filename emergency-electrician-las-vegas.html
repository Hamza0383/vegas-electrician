Emergency Electrical Safety Tips

While waiting for emergency electrical service, follow these safety guidelines to protect yourself and your property. Knowing what to do during electrical emergencies can prevent injuries and additional damage.

When You Smell Burning

If you smell burning near electrical outlets, switches, or your electrical panel, shut off power at the main breaker if you can do so safely. Evacuate if the smell is strong or you see smoke. Call 911 if you suspect an active fire, then call us for emergency electrical service.

Handling Power Outages

If power fails in part of your home, check your circuit breaker panel for tripped breakers. If breakers won't reset or immediately trip again, stop trying to reset them and call for emergency service. The repeated tripping indicates a serious problem that requires professional diagnosis.

Dealing with Sparking Outlets

If an outlet sparks, stop using it immediately. If possible, shut off power to that circuit at the breaker panel. Never use water near electrical sparks. Call for emergency electrical service to diagnose and repair the problem safely.

Electrical Shock Response

If someone receives an electrical shock, call 911 immediately. Do not touch the person if they're still in contact with the electrical source. If you can safely shut off power, do so. Once separated from the electrical source, provide first aid if trained to do so while waiting for emergency responders.

Frequently Asked Questions About Emergency Electrical Service

What qualifies as an electrical emergency?

Electrical emergencies include burning smells from outlets or electrical panels, sparking outlets or switches, complete power loss in your home, electrical shocks from outlets or appliances, exposed wiring, smoking electrical components, and breakers that won't reset. Any situation where you feel electrical safety is compromised should be treated as an emergency requiring immediate professional attention.

How quickly can you respond to electrical emergencies?

We respond to emergency electrical calls as quickly as possible, typically within 1 to 2 hours throughout the Las Vegas Valley. Should I try to fix electrical emergencies myself?

Never attempt to repair electrical emergencies yourself. Emergency electrical situations involve serious hazards including electrocution and fire risks. Shut off power at the main breaker if you can do so safely, but don't attempt repairs without proper training and equipment. Call experienced electricians who have the expertise and tools to handle emergencies safely.

What should I do while waiting for emergency electrical service?

If you smell burning, shut off power at the main breaker if safe to do so and evacuate if necessary. For sparking outlets, stop using them immediately and shut off power to that circuit. Keep family members away from electrical hazards. If you suspect fire, call 911 first, then call us. Don't use water near electrical problems.

Will my homeowner's insurance cover emergency electrical repairs?

Coverage depends on your specific policy and the cause of the electrical emergency. Damage from sudden events like lightning strikes or power surges is often covered. Damage from lack of maintenance or gradual deterioration typically isn't covered. We provide detailed documentation of emergency repairs that you can submit to insurance companies for claims.

Can you prevent electrical emergencies from happening?

Many electrical emergencies can be prevented through regular inspections and prompt attention to warning signs. We recommend electrical inspections every 3 to 5 years for older homes. Preventive maintenance costs far less than emergency repairs and prevents dangerous situations.
