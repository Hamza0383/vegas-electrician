Expert Electrical Wiring and Rewiring Services

Electrical wiring is the foundation of your home's electrical system. Safe, properly installed wiring delivers power throughout your home reliably for decades. However, old wiring deteriorates over time and outdated wiring cannot safely handle modern electrical demands. Electrician Las Vegas provides complete electrical wiring and rewiring services for homes throughout the Las Vegas Valley.

Our experienced electricians handle all types of wiring projects from simple additions and repairs to complete home rewiring. We install modern wiring that meets current electrical codes and safety standards. Every installation is completed professionally with attention to detail that ensures safe, reliable operation for years to come.

Las Vegas homes face unique challenges that accelerate wiring deterioration. The extremely dry desert climate causes wire insulation to crack and become brittle much faster than in humid climates. High electrical demands from air conditioning systems stress wiring beyond original design limits. These factors make electrical wiring problems more common in Las Vegas than many other locations.

Faulty wiring causes thousands of house fires annually. Many of these fires could be prevented through timely rewiring of homes with deteriorating or inadequate electrical systems. Our rewiring services eliminate fire hazards, improve electrical reliability, and provide capacity for modern electrical needs.

Signs Your Home Needs Rewiring

Recognizing warning signs that indicate wiring problems helps you address issues before they become dangerous. Several symptoms suggest your Las Vegas home may need rewiring or significant wiring repairs.

Frequent Circuit Breaker Trips

Circuit breakers that trip frequently indicate your wiring cannot safely handle your electrical demands. While occasional tripping is normal when circuits overload, constant tripping suggests wiring problems or inadequate circuit capacity. Old wiring with deteriorated insulation can cause ground faults that trip breakers repeatedly.

Flickering or Dimming Lights

Lights that flicker or dim when appliances start indicate loose connections or undersized wiring. This symptom shows your electrical system struggles to maintain proper voltage under load. Flickering lights should never be ignored as they often precede more serious electrical problems.

Discolored or Warm Outlets

Outlets that show discoloration, scorching, or feel warm to touch indicate dangerous overheating. This happens when loose connections or overloaded circuits generate excessive heat. Overheating outlets represent active fire hazards requiring immediate professional attention.

Burning Smell Near Outlets or Switches

Any burning smell near electrical components indicates serious problems. Overheating wiring, failing connections, or electrical arcing produces distinctive burning odors. If you smell burning near electrical components, shut off power to the affected area immediately and call for emergency electrical service.

Old Two-Prong Outlets

Homes with two-prong outlets throughout lack modern grounding systems. Ungrounded electrical systems cannot safely power modern electronics and appliances. Three-prong grounded outlets are standard in all modern homes and should be installed during rewiring projects.

Aluminum Wiring

Homes built in the 1960s and 1970s often contain aluminum wiring. Aluminum expands and contracts more than copper, causing connections to loosen over time. Loose connections overheat and create fire hazards. Homes with aluminum wiring should be evaluated by professionals and may need complete rewiring.

Types of Electrical Wiring Problems

Understanding common wiring problems helps you recognize serious issues and appreciate the importance of professional rewiring services.

Deteriorated Wire Insulation

Wire insulation protects against electrical shocks and prevents short circuits. Las Vegas's dry climate accelerates insulation deterioration. Cracked, brittle insulation exposes bare wires that can spark fires or cause shocks. Homes over 40 years old often have significant insulation deterioration requiring rewiring.

Knob-and-Tube Wiring

Knob-and-tube wiring was common in homes built before 1950. This outdated wiring lacks grounding, uses old insulation materials, and cannot safely handle modern electrical loads. Insurance companies often refuse coverage for homes with knob-and-tube wiring. Complete rewiring is the only safe solution for homes with this obsolete wiring.

Undersized Wiring

Many older Las Vegas homes have wiring sized for electrical demands that were common decades ago. Modern homes require larger wire sizes to safely carry increased electrical loads from air conditioning, computers, and large appliances. Undersized wiring overheats under current demands, creating fire hazards.

Improper DIY Wiring

Previous homeowners or unlicensed contractors sometimes perform electrical work that doesn't meet code requirements. Improper wire connections, incorrect wire sizes, and missing safety devices create dangerous conditions hidden behind walls. Professional electricians identify and correct improper wiring during rewiring projects.

The Complete Home Rewiring Process

Complete home rewiring is a significant project that requires careful planning and professional execution. Understanding the process helps you prepare for this important home improvement.

Initial Inspection and Assessment

Rewiring projects begin with our experienced electrician thoroughly inspecting your home's electrical system. We examine the electrical panel, trace circuits, identify wiring types and conditions, check for code violations, and assess the scope of work needed. This inspection provides the information needed to plan your rewiring project accurately.

After inspection, we provide a detailed proposal explaining what work is needed and why. You receive clear pricing that includes all labor, materials, permits, and inspections. We answer your questions and ensure you understand the entire rewiring process.

Planning the Rewiring Project

We plan rewiring projects to minimize disruption to your daily life. Work proceeds room by room or section by section, limiting areas without power at any time. We coordinate with you to schedule work around your needs and preferences.

Planning includes determining circuit layouts, calculating electrical loads, selecting appropriate wire sizes, planning circuit paths through walls and ceilings, and identifying access points for fishing new wiring. Careful planning ensures efficient work and minimizes damage to finished surfaces.

Obtaining Permits and Approvals

Complete rewiring projects require electrical permits from your local building department. We handle all permit applications and ensure the project meets code requirements. Permits typically take a few days to process before work begins.

Removing Old Wiring

We remove old wiring as we install new circuits. Old wiring is disconnected at the panel and removed from accessible locations. Wiring inside walls that cannot be accessed easily is typically abandoned in place after being disconnected. All abandoned wiring is clearly marked at both ends.

Installing New Electrical Wiring

New wiring installation follows current electrical codes and industry best practices. We use appropriate wire sizes for each circuit's load and purpose. All wiring includes proper grounding as required for safety and code compliance.

Fishing Wiring Through Walls

We fish new wiring through existing walls and ceilings using specialized tools and techniques. This process minimizes damage to drywall and finishes. Small access holes are sometimes necessary and are repaired after wiring installation. We protect floors and furnishings throughout the work area.

Installing New Outlets and Switches

As we install new wiring, we replace old outlets and switches with modern three-prong grounded devices. GFCI outlets are installed in kitchens, bathrooms, garages, and outdoor locations as required by current codes. All devices are installed securely with proper wire connections.

Electrical Panel Updates

Rewiring projects often include electrical panel upgrades to provide adequate capacity and circuit spaces for new wiring. We upgrade panels from 100-amp to 200-amp service when needed to support modern electrical demands. New panels include proper circuit protection for all new circuits.

Testing and Inspection

After installation, we test every circuit to verify proper operation. Testing includes voltage measurements, continuity checks, ground fault testing, and load testing. All testing ensures safe, reliable operation before the final inspection.

City electrical inspectors review rewiring projects to verify code compliance. We coordinate all inspections and ensure the work passes on the first attempt. You receive official documentation showing your electrical system is code-compliant.

Partial Rewiring and Circuit Additions

Not all wiring projects require complete home rewiring. We also handle partial rewiring projects and circuit additions that address specific needs or problems.

Room-by-Room Rewiring

Sometimes only certain rooms need rewiring while other areas remain in good condition. We rewire individual rooms or sections of homes when targeted work addresses your specific problems. Room-by-room rewiring costs less than complete rewiring while solving immediate issues.

Kitchen and bathroom rewiring are common partial projects. These rooms have high electrical demands and moisture exposure that accelerates wiring deterioration. Rewiring these areas during renovations ensures safe, code-compliant electrical systems.

Adding New Circuits

Adding circuits expands your electrical system's capacity for new appliances or devices. We install new circuits for home additions, kitchen appliances, electric vehicle chargers, and dedicated circuits for high-power equipment. New circuits include properly sized wiring, appropriate circuit protection, and code-compliant installation.

Garage and Workshop Wiring

Garages and workshops often need additional wiring for tools, equipment, and lighting. We install dedicated circuits sized for power tools, add outlets at convenient locations, and provide proper lighting for work areas. All garage wiring includes GFCI protection as required by electrical codes.

Upgrading to Modern Electrical Standards

Rewiring projects provide opportunities to upgrade your electrical system to current standards that improve safety and functionality.

GFCI and AFCI Protection

Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter (GFCI) outlets protect against electrical shocks in areas near water. We install GFCI outlets in bathrooms, kitchens, garages, outdoor areas, and near pools. Arc Fault Circuit Interrupter (AFCI) breakers detect dangerous arcing and prevent electrical fires. Current codes require AFCI protection in bedrooms, living rooms, and most living areas.

Whole House Surge Protection

We install surge protection devices at electrical panels during rewiring projects. Whole house surge protection defends your entire electrical system against voltage spikes from lightning strikes and power grid problems. This protection prevents damage to sensitive electronics and appliances throughout your home.

Structured Wiring Systems

Modern homes benefit from structured wiring that supports networking, home automation, and entertainment systems. We install Cat6 networking cables, coaxial cables for television, and wiring for smart home devices during rewiring projects. Structured wiring installation during rewiring is much easier than adding it later.

Benefits of Professional Home Rewiring

Investing in professional rewiring provides numerous benefits beyond just fixing electrical problems. Understanding these advantages helps you appreciate the value of this important home improvement.

Improved Safety

The primary benefit of rewiring is eliminating electrical fire hazards. Regular electrical inspections identify when rewiring is needed. New wiring with intact insulation, proper connections, and adequate capacity dramatically reduces fire risk. Modern circuit protection devices including GFCI and AFCI breakers provide additional safety that old electrical systems lack.

Increased Electrical Capacity

New wiring provides capacity for modern electrical demands. You can operate air conditioning, kitchen appliances, electronics, and other devices without overloading circuits or tripping breakers. Adequate electrical capacity makes your home more comfortable and functional.

Higher Home Value

Updated electrical systems increase home values significantly. Buyers appreciate knowing they won't face immediate rewiring expenses. Homes with modern electrical systems sell faster and command higher prices than comparable homes with outdated wiring.

Lower Insurance Costs

Some insurance companies reduce premiums for homes with updated electrical systems. Old wiring increases fire risk, leading to higher insurance rates. Updated wiring demonstrates reduced risk and may qualify for insurance discounts.

Code Compliance

Rewiring brings your electrical system into compliance with current codes. Code-compliant systems pass inspections more easily during home sales. Compliance also ensures your electrical system meets modern safety standards that protect your family and property.

Understanding what to expect regarding timeline and costs helps you plan your rewiring project appropriately.

Complete home rewiring typically takes one to two weeks depending on home size and complexity. We work efficiently to minimize the time your home is disrupted. Power remains available most of the time, with only brief interruptions for critical connection work.

Partial rewiring projects take less time. Single room rewiring may be completed in one to three days. Circuit additions are often completed in a single day. We provide accurate timeline estimates based on your specific project scope.

Frequently Asked Questions About Electrical Wiring and Rewiring

How much does it cost to rewire a house in Las Vegas?

Complete home rewiring typically costs $8,000 to $15,000 for average-sized homes in Las Vegas. The cost varies based on home size, accessibility, existing wiring condition, and whether walls need to be opened. Larger homes or those requiring extensive repairs cost more. Partial rewiring of specific rooms costs $1,500 to $3,500 per room. We provide detailed estimates after inspecting your home.

How long does it take to rewire a house?

Complete home rewiring typically takes 1 to 2 weeks depending on home size and complexity. We work room by room to minimize disruption, keeping power available in most areas while we work. Partial rewiring projects take less time, often 1 to 3 days per room. We provide accurate timelines after assessing your specific project.

Do I need to move out during rewiring?

Most homeowners stay in their homes during rewiring projects. We work room by room, maintaining power in areas we're not working on. You'll need to relocate items from work areas and tolerate some disruption, but complete relocation usually isn't necessary. For extensive projects, some homeowners prefer staying elsewhere temporarily.

How do I know if my house needs rewiring?

Signs your home needs rewiring include frequent circuit breaker trips, flickering lights, burning smells near outlets, discolored outlets, two-prong outlets throughout, aluminum wiring, and homes over 40 years old. If your home was built before 1980 and hasn't been rewired, inspection is recommended. We provide comprehensive electrical inspections that identify wiring problems.

Will rewiring damage my walls?

Rewiring requires some wall access, but we minimize damage by fishing wiring through existing walls when possible. Small access holes are sometimes necessary and are repaired after wiring installation. We protect floors and furnishings, contain dust and debris, and clean thoroughly. Most wall damage is minor and easily repaired.

Can I rewire my house myself?

We strongly advise against DIY home rewiring. Rewiring requires extensive electrical knowledge, proper tools, code compliance expertise, and inspection coordination. Improper wiring creates serious fire and shock hazards. experienced electricians ensure safe installations that meet codes and pass inspections. The cost of professional rewiring is justified by safety and quality.

Does rewiring increase home value?

Yes, updated electrical systems significantly increase home values. Buyers appreciate knowing they won't face immediate rewiring expenses. Homes with modern electrical systems sell faster and command higher prices than comparable homes with outdated wiring. Updated wiring also makes homes easier to insure and may qualify for insurance discounts. Updated electrical systems also make it easier to add EV chargers and other modern electrical needs.

What type of wiring is used for rewiring?

Modern rewiring uses copper wire with proper insulation rated for residential use. We install wire sizes appropriate for each circuit's load, typically 14-gauge for 15-amp circuits and 12-gauge for 20-amp circuits. All wiring includes proper grounding as required by current codes. We use quality materials rated for decades of reliable service.
