What Is a GFCI Outlet and Why Does Las Vegas Require Them?

How GFCI Outlets Prevent Electrical Shock

A standard outlet keeps running power even when something goes wrong. A GFCI outlet doesn't. It watches the current flow. The moment it spots a problem, it cuts the power. That happens in under a second.

Near water, in garages, outdoors — this type of protection is the last line of defense.

Where GFCI Outlets Are Required in Las Vegas Homes

Kitchens — Near Sinks & Countertops

Every spot within 6 feet of a sink needs protection. That's code here. That covers most countertop outlets in a kitchen. We install at every required spot. We check the wiring behind it before we close it up.

Bathrooms, Garages & Basements

Every bathroom outlet needs GFCI protection. Garages too — the whole garage, not just near a sink. Unfinished basements and crawl spaces also require GFCI under current code.

GFCI Breaker vs. GFCI Outlet

A GFCI breaker protects every outlet on that circuit. A GFCI outlet protects itself and anything wired downstream.

In most homes here, GFCI outlets are the right call. Easier to reset. Simpler to fix. We'll tell you which option suits your situation.

Troubleshooting

GFCI Outlet Problems We Fix in Las Vegas

GFCI Outlet That Won't Reset

You press reset. Nothing happens. Or it resets and trips right away again.

This usually means there's still a fault on the circuit. In an appliance, another outlet, or the wiring. We find the fault first. Then we fix it. Just replacing the outlet won't solve it if the real cause is still there.

GFCI That Trips Often or at Random

A GFCI that trips all the time is telling you something. Moisture in a connection. A failing outlet. An appliance drawing odd current. We test the circuit and trace the cause. Every GFCI outlet installation Las Vegas diagnostic starts with finding the real problem — not swapping the device.

Wiring Issues Behind Non-Working GFCI Outlets

Sometimes the GFCI itself is fine. The wiring behind it isn't. Loose wires, reversed connections, a missing ground — all cause GFCI failures. We open the box, check the wiring, and fix what's actually wrong.
