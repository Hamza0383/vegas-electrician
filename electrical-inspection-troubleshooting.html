Comprehensive Electrical Inspections

Professional electrical inspections examine your entire electrical system to identify problems, code violations, and safety hazards. Our thorough inspections provide detailed information about your electrical system's condition and needed improvements.

Our electrical inspections cover every component of your electrical system. Professional Electrical Troubleshooting

When electrical problems occur, accurate diagnosis is essential for effective repairs. Our troubleshooting services identify root causes rather than just treating symptoms.

Common Electrical Problems We Diagnose

We troubleshoot all types of electrical problems including flickering or dimming lights, outlets that don't work, switches that malfunction, circuit breakers that trip repeatedly, burning smells from electrical components, buzzing or humming sounds, and intermittent electrical issues. Each symptom provides clues that help us locate underlying problems. Some problems require emergency electrical service for immediate safety.

Electrical problems often have multiple possible causes. Flickering lights might result from loose connections, overloaded circuits, failing fixtures, or problems with utility service. Our systematic troubleshooting identifies the actual cause so repairs fix problems permanently.

Our Troubleshooting Process

Troubleshooting begins with gathering information about symptoms. We ask when problems started, what makes them better or worse, and what you've already tried. This information helps us develop diagnostic plans and prepare appropriate testing equipment.

Visual Inspection and Testing

We start troubleshooting with careful visual inspection. Many electrical problems show visible signs including discolored outlets, loose connections, damaged wiring, or improper installations. Visual inspection often reveals obvious problems before detailed testing begins.

After visual inspection, we use specialized testing equipment to measure voltage, current, and resistance throughout electrical systems. These measurements identify problems like voltage drops, ground faults, short circuits, and poor connections. Testing equipment provides objective data that guides our diagnostic process.

Circuit Tracing and Isolation

When problems affect multiple outlets or areas, we trace circuits to understand how they're connected. Circuit tracing helps us isolate problems to specific sections of circuits. This isolation narrows down where problems exist, making final diagnosis faster and more accurate.

We use tone generators and tracers that identify circuit paths through walls and ceilings. These tools allow us to map circuits without damaging walls or making assumptions about wiring routes.

Load Testing and Analysis

Some electrical problems only occur under specific load conditions. We perform load testing that simulates normal usage while monitoring electrical values. Load testing reveals problems that don't show up during no-load testing.

Overloaded circuits cause many electrical problems in Las Vegas homes. Air conditioning systems, kitchen appliances, and other high-power devices stress circuits beyond their capacity. Load analysis determines if circuits can safely handle their demands or need upgrades.

Troubleshooting Specific Electrical Issues

Different electrical problems require specialized diagnostic approaches. Our experience troubleshooting Las Vegas electrical systems helps us solve issues efficiently.

Flickering and Dimming Lights

Flickering lights indicate loose connections, overloaded circuits, or voltage problems. We measure voltage at affected fixtures, check connections throughout the circuit, and verify that circuits aren't overloaded. Thermal imaging often reveals loose connections that cause flickering.

Lights that dim when appliances start indicate voltage drops from inadequate wiring or overloaded circuits. We measure voltage under load and calculate circuit capacity to determine if upgrades are needed.

Dead Outlets and Non-Working Circuits

Outlets that don't work may have tripped GFCI protection, blown fuses, tripped breakers, or wiring problems. We test systematically from the electrical panel to the affected outlet, identifying exactly where power stops flowing.

Sometimes outlets appear dead but actually have loose connections that create intermittent contact. We check all connections and verify proper wire termination at outlets and junction boxes.

Breakers That Trip Repeatedly

Circuit breakers trip for specific reasons including overloaded circuits, short circuits, ground faults, or failing breakers. We determine why breakers trip through load testing, insulation testing, and breaker evaluation.

Old breakers sometimes weaken and trip at lower currents than their ratings. We test breaker trip points to verify they provide proper protection. Breakers that trip below their ratings need replacement.

Intermittent Electrical Problems

Intermittent problems are challenging to diagnose because they come and go. We ask detailed questions about when problems occur, what conditions trigger them, and any patterns you've noticed. This information helps us recreate conditions that cause problems.

Thermal expansion and contraction from Las Vegas temperature extremes can cause intermittent connection problems. Connections that work when cool may fail when hot. We check connections under various temperature conditions when diagnosing intermittent issues.

Electrical Safety Inspections

Safety inspections focus specifically on identifying electrical hazards that threaten people and property. These inspections are more detailed than general inspections and use specialized testing to find hidden dangers.

Fire Hazard Identification

Electrical fires cause thousands of home fires annually. Our safety inspections identify fire hazards including overloaded circuits, loose connections, deteriorated wiring, improper installations, and outdated electrical components. Finding and correcting these hazards prevents devastating electrical fires.

Thermal imaging reveals hot connections that indicate fire hazards. These hot spots show where connections are failing and generating dangerous heat. Correcting hot connections before they cause fires protects your family and property.

Shock Hazard Detection

We test for shock hazards including missing or improper grounding, damaged wiring insulation, wet location outlets without GFCI protection, and reversed polarity connections. These hazards create conditions where touching electrical components or appliances can cause serious shocks.

GFCI outlet testing verifies these safety devices function correctly. Failed GFCI outlets provide no protection against electrical shocks. We test every GFCI outlet and replace any that don't trip properly.

Code Compliance Verification

Electrical codes exist to ensure safe installations. Our inspections identify code violations that create safety hazards or prevent proper electrical operation. Common violations include improper wire sizing, missing junction box covers, inadequate circuit protection, and installations that don't meet current standards.

We explain code violations clearly and recommend corrections that bring your electrical system into compliance. Code-compliant electrical systems are safer and easier to insure.

Thermal Imaging Inspections

Thermal imaging cameras detect heat invisible to visual inspection. This technology reveals electrical problems before they cause failures or fires.

How Thermal Imaging Works

Thermal cameras detect infrared radiation that all objects emit. Hotter objects emit more infrared radiation and appear brighter in thermal images. Electrical connections that are failing, overloaded circuits, and components that are about to fail all generate excessive heat visible in thermal images.

We scan electrical panels, junction boxes, and circuits with thermal cameras during inspections. Hot spots indicate problems that need attention. This preventive approach finds issues before they cause emergency situations.

Benefits of Thermal Inspections

Thermal imaging finds problems that visual inspection misses. Loose connections hidden inside electrical panels, overheated wiring behind walls, and circuit overloads all show up clearly in thermal images. Finding these problems early prevents failures and fires.

Thermal inspections are non-invasive and don't require shutting off power or disassembling electrical systems. We can scan your entire electrical system quickly without disrupting your daily activities.

What Pre-Purchase Inspections Cover
