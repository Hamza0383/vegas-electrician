Problems We Fix Every Day

Breakers trip when the AC starts. The compressor pulls a big surge at startup. If your panel is already loaded, something trips. Usually means an undersized panel or a bad circuit. As the go-to electrician Las Vegas residents call for panel issues, we sort this out fast.

Lights flicker in summer. A quick flicker when the AC kicks on is normal. Flickering in multiple rooms is not. That usually means a loose neutral at the meter base. Get it checked today.

Burning smell near the panel. Shut the main off and call us right away. Overheating inside a panel does not wait. Bad connection, failing breaker, corroded bus bar — we find it and fix it.

Outdoor outlets dead after monsoon rains. Water trips GFCI protection. If the reset button does not fix it, the housing is cracked or corroded. We replace it the same day.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my panel is overloaded?

Breakers tripping when the AC starts and lights dimming when appliances kick on are the clearest signs. We run a load calculation on every visit.

Do I need a permit for electrical work in Las Vegas?

Yes — for panel upgrades, new circuits, rewiring, and EV charger installs. We pull the permits. Your work is legal and done to code.

How long does a panel upgrade take?

One day for most 200-amp upgrades. You will be without power for about four to six hours.

My lights flicker every summer. Is that a problem?

A brief flicker when the AC starts is usually fine. Frequent flickering in multiple rooms means get it checked. It is almost always a loose neutral or a panel near its limit.
