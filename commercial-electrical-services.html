Electrical Panel Upgrades for Growing Businesses

Commercial electrical panels distribute power throughout your facility. An undersized or outdated panel limits your ability to add equipment and expand operations. Many commercial buildings in Las Vegas have electrical panels that cannot support modern business needs. Panel upgrades provide the capacity required for current operations and future growth.

Signs your commercial property needs a panel upgrade include breakers that trip frequently, inability to add new equipment without overloading circuits, a panel that feels warm to touch, or plans to expand operations that require additional electrical capacity. These indicators mean your electrical system cannot safely handle your business demands.

We assess your current electrical system and calculate the capacity needed to support your operations. This analysis includes existing equipment loads, planned additions, and reserve capacity for future growth. Most commercial panel upgrades involve increasing service size from 100 or 200 amps to 400 or 600 amps, depending on your facility size and requirements.

Our commercial panel upgrade process includes coordinating with the utility company, pulling all required permits, scheduling work to minimize business disruption, installing the new panel and service entrance, reconnecting all circuits, testing the complete system, and obtaining final inspection approval. We can often perform upgrade work during off-hours to avoid interrupting business operations.

A properly sized electrical panel eliminates power interruptions and supports business growth. You can add equipment, expand operations, and install new technologies without electrical limitations. The upgrade also improves safety by ensuring your electrical system can handle its load without overheating or creating fire hazards.

Energy Efficiency Upgrades for Commercial Properties

Energy costs represent significant operating expenses for commercial properties. Energy-efficient electrical upgrades reduce consumption and lower utility bills. Electrician Las Vegas helps businesses reduce energy costs through electrical improvements and upgrades.

LED Lighting Retrofits

LED lighting retrofits provide the fastest return on investment. Replacing old fluorescent or metal halide lighting with LED fixtures reduces energy consumption by 50-75%. LED lights also last much longer, reducing maintenance costs. We calculate potential savings and help you understand the payback period for lighting upgrades.

Lighting Controls and Automation

Lighting controls reduce energy consumption without affecting lighting quality. Occupancy sensors turn lights off in unoccupied spaces. Daylight harvesting systems dim electric lights when natural light is available. Time controls ensure lights operate only when needed. These controls typically reduce lighting energy use by 30-50%.

Variable Frequency Drives

Variable frequency drives on motors reduce energy consumption for HVAC systems and other motor-driven equipment. VFDs adjust motor speed to match actual loads rather than running at full speed constantly. Energy savings can reach 40-50% depending on the application.

Power Factor Correction

Power factor correction reduces electrical demand charges on utility bills. Poor power factor causes higher bills even without using more energy. We install power factor correction equipment that improves efficiency and reduces demand charges. The savings often pay for the equipment within two years.

Generator Installation for Business Continuity

Power outages can devastate business operations. Standby generators provide automatic backup power that keeps critical systems running during outages. Electrician Las Vegas installs and maintains commercial generators for businesses that cannot afford downtime.

We help you determine the right generator size for your needs. This involves calculating the electrical load for critical systems, determining fuel type, and selecting between air-cooled and liquid-cooled generators. Generator sizing must account for starting currents of motors and other equipment with high inrush demands.

Generator installation includes preparing the installation pad, setting the generator, connecting fuel supply, installing transfer switches, connecting to electrical panels, and testing the complete system. Transfer switches automatically start generators and switch power sources when outages occur. The entire process happens automatically without human intervention.

We install both natural gas and diesel generators depending on your facility requirements and fuel availability. Natural gas generators connect to utility gas lines for unlimited fuel supply. Diesel generators provide independence from utility services but require fuel storage and delivery.

Regular generator maintenance ensures reliable operation when you need backup power. Maintenance includes exercising generators under load, changing oil and filters, testing transfer switches, inspecting fuel systems, and verifying automatic operation. We provide generator maintenance contracts that keep your backup power system ready for emergencies.
